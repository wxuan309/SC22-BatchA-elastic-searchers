The Why

According to the CDC, diabetes is a chronic disorder that impacts how a person’s body converts food into energy. Diabetes slows down or stops the production of insulin, increasing the amount of glucose, or blood sugar. This increase leads to heart disease, vision loss, and other serious health-problems. Our objective is to spread awareness on the topic of diabetes. It is something that a person can carry through their life without knowing. The purpose of our website is to aid people in self-evaluation to see whether they may or may not be more prone to having diabetes.

Exploration Of Our Data

Our data is derived from a 2019 Diabetes data set from Kaggle. The evaluated factors include: Age, Gender, Family Diabetes, High Blood Pressure, Prediabetes, Physical Active, BMI, Smoking, Alcohol, Sleep, Sound Sleep, Regular Medicine, Junk Food, Stress, BP level, Pregnancies, Urination, and Diabetic.

Our Models

How it all works, explanations and more on our models.

Logistic regression
Logistic regression estimates the probability of an event occurring, such as voted or didn't vote, based on a given dataset of independent variables. Since the outcome is a probability, the dependent variable is bounded between 0 and 1.


Random forest
Random forests or random decision forests is an ensemble learning method for classification, regression and other tasks that operates by constructing a multitude of decision trees at training time. For classification tasks, the output of the random forest is the class selected by most trees.


Support Vector Machine(SVM)
Support-Vector Machines are supervised learning models with associated learning algorithms that analyze data for classification and regression problems. They use hyperplanes or lines that separate the data into two categories.
